# TX-11: August Pfluger — Financial Disclosure Summary

August Pfluger is the Republican incumbent representing Texas's 11th Congressional District, in office since January 2021 and, since January 2025, chair of the Republican Study Committee — the largest ideological caucus in the House. He also sits on the Energy and Commerce Committee, a detail worth keeping in mind below. Redistricting effective for the 2026 election reorients TX-11 away from its traditional Permian Basin/Big Bend footprint and toward suburban Austin, adding parts of Travis and Williamson counties (North Austin, Pflugerville, Cedar Park, Leander) while dropping the stretch toward Killeen and Fort Cavazos. The Permian Basin core, including Midland and San Angelo, stays in the district.

Three FEC committees report activity for this filing period (01/01/2025–06/30/2026): his principal campaign committee, **August Pfluger for Congress** (C00719294); a four-way joint fundraising committee, **Pfluger Victory Committee** (C00753913), which splits proceeds among the campaign, Pfluger's own leadership PAC (**Raptor PAC**, C00749481), the **NRCC**, and the **House Conservatives Fund**; and **Pfriends of Pfluger** (C00840033), which took in a single $5,000 contribution from E-PAC and refunded it in full — net zero activity, effectively dormant this period. Combined, the three reported **$4,244,144.74** in itemized receipts and **$5,801,899.19** in itemized disbursements (the disbursement figure runs higher because $3.17 million of it is the JFC redistributing pooled money to its four participants, not new spending). The campaign committee ended the period with **$2,881,724.29** cash on hand (up from $2,324,105.54) and no debt; the JFC ended with $293,100.00 (up from $64,727.42), also debt-free.

Outside Texas, Washington, DC ($428,300) and Virginia ($202,190) — both dense with the D.C.-based PAC and energy-lobbying corps — outrank every state but Texas itself.

## Major Spending

Excluding the $3.17 million in JFC-to-participant transfers (not "spending" in the ads/staff/travel sense — see Methodology), real outlays run **$2,633,893.06**. Administrative/Salary/Overhead leads at $1,307,170.46, followed by Solicitation and Fundraising at $719,158.50 — together more than three-quarters of all non-transfer spending, typical for an operation this focused on JFC-driven donor acquisition.

**American Express** is the largest single payee outside the transfer network at $652,300.18, booked mostly as lump card payments. Rather than treat that as an opaque total, the analysis tool traces the 1,817 memo-coded sub-transactions that back-reference these lump payments (94% of the $992,708.13 in card payments across all committees is itemized this way) to real vendors: **American Airlines** ($71,541), **The Capital Grille** ($34,006), **Del Frisco's Double Eagle Steakhouse** ($27,582), **Oceanaire** ($19,977), and **Fiola Mare** ($10,420) — all high-end D.C. restaurants — plus **Hilton Hotel - DC** ($19,571), the **Capitol Hill Club** ($12,851, the private Capitol Hill club for House Republicans), and car services **Savoya** and **BLS Limo Group** (a combined $24,272). Two staffers, **Grace Dunham** and **Caroline Brennan**, were also paid tens of thousands through the card rather than payroll checks ($33,526 and $27,000 respectively, on top of $124,685 and $148,627 already booked to them directly as top payees).

**Targeted Victory** (Arlington, VA), the campaign's digital/fundraising vendor, took $342,925.28 — split between $267,920 in Solicitation/Fundraising fees and $75,005 in Advertising, reflecting the JFC's online small-dollar and WinRed-adjacent fundraising infrastructure. **NRCC** received $863,350.89 total, but only $185,500 of that was a direct political contribution; the remaining $677,851 was its cut of JFC transfer proceeds. **CFS Compliance** ($95,037) and **Hooks Solutions, LLC** ($88,575, Washington, DC) round out the compliance/fundraising-consulting spend, alongside $34,800 to **Ragnar Research Partners** for polling and $43,959 to the **Stein Eriksen Lodge** (Park City, UT) — presumably a donor retreat, given the same period includes $37,819 to the St. Joe Resort in Panama City Beach, FL, both booked as Solicitation/Fundraising or Campaign Event spend.

## Takeaways

1. **One Midland oilman personally accounts for 15 cents of every dollar raised.** Syed Javaid Anwar's $642,100 — funneled entirely through the JFC, which is how a single donor can legally exceed the per-committee contribution limit — is more than the campaign's next 24 donors combined. That's an unusually concentrated funding relationship for a sitting committee chair.
2. **Midland, not the district's population centers, is the financial engine.** A city of roughly 135,000 supplied over a third of all itemized money — more than the rest of Texas combined. The donor base reads like a directory of Permian Basin oil-and-gas leadership (Double Eagle Energy, BC Operating, Western Shamrock, Natura Resources), tightly aligned with the district's energy economy even as its population center shifts toward Austin's suburbs.
3. **His own portfolio moved into the same sector as his top donors, on the same committee that oversees it.** A March 2026 Periodic Transaction Report shows six new stock positions of $15,001–$50,000 each, four of them Permian-Basin-adjacent oil-and-gas plays: Viper Energy (a Diamondback Energy subsidiary drilling in the Permian), Kimbell Royalty Partners and Dorchester Minerals (both oil-and-gas royalty aggregators), and Enterprise Products Partners (midstream pipelines) — alongside Berkshire Hathaway and Amerco. Pfluger sits on the Energy and Commerce Committee. Nothing here suggests a disclosure violation — STOCK Act reporting is exactly what surfaced this — but the overlap between his donor industry, his committee assignment, and his personal buys is the kind of alignment worth watching.
4. **Leadership-track spending, not just district spending.** The JFC's fourth participant, the House Conservatives Fund (a Washington-based leadership PAC, distinct from Pfluger's own Raptor PAC), plus the Capitol Hill Club dues and a cluster of upscale D.C. steakhouse charges on the card, point to an operation as focused on House GOP leadership relationships as on TX-11 itself — consistent with his role chairing the Republican Study Committee.
5. **No 2025 Annual Financial Disclosure is in this data yet — and that's expected, not a gap.** Pfluger filed for (and received) a 90-day extension on 04/14/2026, pushing his original 2025 FD's due date to 08/13/2026, after this analysis was run. Only Periodic Transaction Reports and the extension request itself are on file here; his net-worth and outside-income picture can't be assessed until that FD posts.
