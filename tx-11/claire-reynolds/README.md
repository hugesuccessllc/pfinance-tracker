## Takeaways

1. **Redistricting, not just party affiliation, appears to be why this race exists.** TX-11's new lines pull in a strip of North Austin, most of Pflugerville, and small pieces of Round Rock, Cedar Park, and Leander — turning a historically Midland/Odessa/San Angelo seat into one that also reaches Travis County. Reynolds's donor base, campaign vendors, and at least one campaign event (Pflugerville) all sit inside that newly added slice, while her travel spending (Midland, San Angelo hotels) covers the district's older western core. The map redraw looks like the actual trigger for her candidacy, not an afterthought.

2. **Ballot access itself was a five-figure-adjacent cost before any voter outreach began.** The $3,125.00 filing fee plus $2,200.00 in VAN data access — both paid to the Texas Democratic Party — total $5,325.00, nearly 20% of all itemized disbursements. For a campaign this size, the structural cost of simply getting on the ballot and accessing party voter data is a significant early expense.

3. **The donor base clusters in tech and cybersecurity — which fits the new Austin-suburb portion of the district, not the old rural one.** Contributors include a Google product manager, a UT Austin cybersecurity risk analyst, an OPSWAT engineer, and HD Moore — a prominent figure in the cybersecurity industry and an executive at runZero, the same company that employs the candidate's spouse. That's a natural donor pool for the Pflugerville/North Austin corridor TX-11 is gaining, and the runZero tie is worth noting as a household connection even though it's a modest, disclosed amount.

4. **The candidate gave up a $120,000 salary to run.** Her House Ethics disclosure (Schedule C) shows $120,000 in prior-year salary from the State Bar of Texas, dropped to $0 for the current filing period — she left her job as an attorney to campaign full-time. Household assets are comfortable (retirement accounts in the $100,001–$250,000 range, a jointly owned Terlingua rental property valued at $250,001–$500,000), but the campaign's own cash position is thin: $46,790.25 on hand against $3,317.10 in committee debt.

5. **Self-funding is present but modest.** The candidate contributed roughly $3,600 of her own money — about 10% of itemized receipts — alongside a $1,200 contribution from a Missouri donor sharing her surname, suggesting some family support beyond the candidate's own pocket.

- TX-11's redrawn district lines (the North Austin/Pflugerville/Round Rock/Cedar Park/Leander additions referenced above) are not derivable from the FEC or House Ethics filings in this directory — that context was supplied directly by the user and reflected here rather than sourced from `$CANDIDATE_DIR/`.

The actual committee ID (`C00929711`) was confirmed from the `committee_id` column inside the CSVs themselves, and the directory was renamed to match the convention already used for Pfluger's data. - Two House Ethics filings exist for this candidate: an initial handwritten "New Member/Candidate" Form B (Filing ID #8221333, filed 02/06/2026, covering a shorter/unspecified period) and a later, digitally signed, typed "Candidate Report" (Filing ID #10076507, filed 05/11/2026, covering 01/01/2025–04/15/2026). Those pages were excluded from this analysis entirely; nothing on them is reflected in this document.
- Standard checks from the tool's header comments were run: no duplicate or competing `transaction_id` values were found in either schedule, and all non-`N` `amendment_indicator` rows were type `A` ("ADD"), not corrections — so no netting adjustment was needed beyond what the script already does by design (summing signed amounts rather than filtering by sign, and excluding memo-coded rows per the documented Schedule A/B distinction).
